--- title: "Correcting the Ideal Gas Law" ---

The Van der Waals Equation

(P + a·n²/V²)(V − nb) = nRT

This equation makes two corrections to the ideal gas law:

  • + a·n²/V², corrects for intermolecular attraction reducing the measured pressure
  • − nb, corrects for the excluded volume of the molecules themselves

Let's compare. On the right are two simulations running side by side.

Top: Ideal gas (no attraction, point particles)
Bottom: Real gas (attraction + finite size)

The pressure graph shows both: ideal (first trace) vs real (second trace).

Change the temperature and see how the difference changes.

Cool the gas down.

At low temperature, the real gas pressure drops significantly below ideal, the attractions dominate. If you go cold enough, the real gas will start to condense into clumps!

Heat it up high.

At high temperature, the two pressures converge, fast-moving particles are barely affected by the weak attractions. Hot real gases behave nearly ideally.
