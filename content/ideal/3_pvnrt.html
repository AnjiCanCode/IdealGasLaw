Putting It Together: PV = nRT

PV = nRT

This is the ideal gas law. It combines everything we've seen:

  • P — pressure (how hard particles push on walls)
  • V — volume (how much space they have)
  • n — amount of gas (number of particles)
  • R — a universal constant
  • T — temperature (how fast they move)

Use both sliders to change volume and temperature simultaneously.

Add more particles with the create tool to increase n.

More particles → higher pressure, just as the equation predicts.

The beauty of PV = nRT is that it predicts the pressure of any ideal gas if you know V, n, and T. No matter how you change the conditions — squeeze the box, heat it up, add particles — the relationship holds.
