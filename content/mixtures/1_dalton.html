Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures

What happens when you mix different gases together?

On the right, black particles and red particles share the same box. They're both ideal gases — no attraction, just bouncing.

The total pressure on the walls is the sum of the partial pressures from each gas:

Ptotal = PA + PB

This is Dalton's Law: each gas contributes pressure as if the other gas wasn't there.

Try adding more of one type using the create tool. The total pressure increases, but each gas type independently contributes its share.

This works because ideal gas particles don't care what's around them — a nitrogen molecule hits the wall just as hard whether it's surrounded by other nitrogen or by oxygen molecules.
