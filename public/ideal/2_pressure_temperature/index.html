Pressure & Temperature: Gay-Lussac's Law

Now let's keep the volume fixed and change the temperature instead.

Temperature controls how fast the particles move. Use the slider below to crank up the heat.

Increase the temperature and watch the particles on the right.

The particles are moving much faster! They hit the walls harder and more often.

Now cool them back down.

Slower particles → gentler collisions → lower pressure.

This direct relationship is Gay-Lussac's Law:

P ∝ T   (at constant volume)

Double the temperature, double the pressure. The ratio P/T stays constant when volume doesn't change.
