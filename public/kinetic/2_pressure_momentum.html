Pressure IS Momentum Transfer

Let's zoom in on what pressure really means at the particle level.

Here's a single, large particle. Throw it at a wall!

See that spike in the graph? That's the momentum transfer — the particle hits the wall, reverses direction, and pushes the wall outward in the process.

A harder throw = a bigger spike. Try throwing it faster!

Now push the particle against the wall and hold it there.

Sustained pushing = sustained pressure. With a single particle you get spikes, but with many particles hitting the walls continuously, the spikes average out into a steady pressure reading.

This is exactly what gas pressure is: the average force per unit area from countless molecular collisions, happening billions of times per second.
