Temperature IS Motion

Temperature isn't some mysterious abstract quantity. It's simply a measure of how fast the particles are moving.

T = ⅔ × (average kinetic energy) / kB

Each particle has kinetic energy = ½mv². Temperature is just the average of all these energies.

The graph shows the average kinetic energy (proportional to temperature). Use the slider to change the thermostat temperature.

Crank the temperature up high.

The particles are zipping around! High temperature = high average speed.

Now bring it way down.

At low temperature, the particles barely move. If you could reach absolute zero (T = 0), they would stop entirely.

This is the kinetic theory of gases: temperature is motion, and motion is temperature.
